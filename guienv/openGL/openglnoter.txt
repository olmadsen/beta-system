(* Note: Beta only has a few atom types:
 * real, integer, shortint, char.
 * OpenGL operates with a large number of types, these
 * are mapped as follows:
 * |----------------------------------------------------------
 * |c-type        | openGL       | Beta        | size in bytes
 * |---------------------------------------------------------
 * |void*         | GLvoid       | integer     |  4
 * |unsigned char | GLboolean    | char        |  1
 * |signed char   | GLbyte       | char        |  1
 * |short         | GLshort      | shortint    |  2
 * |int           | GLint        | integer     |  4
 * |unsigned char | GLubyte      | char        |  1
 * |unsigned int  | GLuint       | integer     |  4
 * |int	          | GLsizei      | integer     |  4
 * |float         | GLfloat      | real        |  8
 * |float         | GLclampf     | real        |  8
 * |double        | GLdouble     | real        |  8
 * |double        | GLclampd     | real        |  8
 * |unsigned int  | GLbitfield   | integer     |  4
 * 
 * Notice that GLfloat are done with reals. These reals
 * are converted to floats i a c program, just before
 * actually calling the openGL routines. This is of 
 * course expensive. Use the GLdouble routines when 
 * they are available.
 *)
